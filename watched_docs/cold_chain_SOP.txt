# Cold Chain Standard Operating Procedures (SOP)
## Version 2.3 - January 2026

---

## §1. Temperature Control Requirements

### §1.1 Temperature Ranges by Product Type

All refrigerated cargo must be maintained within specified temperature ranges:

- **Dairy Products**: +2°C to +10°C
- **Frozen Meat**: -18°C to -12°C
- **Seafood**: -1°C to +2°C
- **Fresh Vegetables**: +7°C to +10°C
- **Fresh Fruits**: +8°C to +12°C
- **Vaccines**: +2°C to +8°C
- **Pharmaceuticals**: +2°C to +8°C
- **Ice Cream**: -25°C to -18°C
- **Flowers**: +2°C to +8°C

### §1.2 Temperature Monitoring

Temperature must be monitored continuously with readings recorded at minimum every 5 minutes. Sensors must be calibrated monthly and replaced every 12 months.

---

## §2. Sensor Verification Protocols

### §2.1 Primary Sensor Check

When a temperature alert is triggered, operators must immediately verify the reading using the following protocol:

1. Check redundant sensor on the same pallet (if available)
2. Visually inspect sensor placement and condition
3. Compare with historical readings from last 30 minutes
4. Check for door open events in the system log

**Time Requirement**: Verification must be completed within 2 minutes of alert.

### §2.2 Sensor Calibration

If sensors show discrepancy >1°C, recalibration is required before continuing operations.

---

## §3. Temperature Excursion Response

### §3.1 Minor Excursion (0-2°C above threshold)

For temperature elevations of 0-2°C above specified range:

1. Check reefer unit status immediately
2. Verify door closure
3. Monitor for 10 minutes
4. If temperature does not decrease, proceed to §3.2

### §3.2 Moderate Excursion (2-5°C above threshold)

For temperature elevations of 2-5°C above specified range:

1. **IMMEDIATE ACTION**: Check compressor status and refrigerant levels
2. Alert reefer technician within 5 minutes
3. If temperature exceeds threshold for >15 minutes, evaluate diversion options (see §4)
4. Document incident with photos and GPS coordinates
5. Notify customer service if delay >30 minutes expected

**Critical Timeframe**: Action must begin within 5 minutes of alert.

### §3.3 Critical Excursion (>5°C above threshold)

For temperature elevations exceeding 5°C above specified range:

1. **URGENT**: Immediately stop vehicle in safe location
2. Alert fleet manager and reefer technician simultaneously
3. Initiate emergency diversion protocols (see §4.3)
4. Document complete event timeline
5. Prepare for possible cargo salvage or disposal

**Critical Timeframe**: Vehicle must stop within 10 minutes.

## §4. Diversion Decision Protocols

### §4.1 Diversion Assessment Criteria

Diversion should be considered when:

- Temperature exceeds threshold by >3°C for >20 minutes
- Reefer unit failure confirmed with >2 hour repair time
- Risk of spoilage exceeds 40% based on cargo value
- Expected value saved exceeds diversion cost + ₹10,000 buffer

### §4.2 Diversion Decision Matrix

**Decision Formula**:
```
Expected Loss (Continue) = P(spoilage) × Cargo Value
Expected Loss (Divert) = Diversion Cost + Residual Risk × Cargo Value

If Expected Loss (Continue) > Expected Loss (Divert) → DIVERT
```

**Approval Requirements**:
- Diversions costing <₹25,000: Fleet Manager approval
- Diversions costing ₹25,000-₹100,000: Operations Director approval
- Diversions costing >₹100,000: VP Operations approval

### §4.3 Emergency Diversion Hubs (Automated Sync)

### §4.3 Automated Diversion Hub Management

The system maintains a dynamic database of approved cold storage facilities and reefer repair stations. When a diversion is required, the **Hub Manager** automatically identifies the optimal facility based on:

1.  **Proximity**: Real-time distance from the truck's current GPS location.
2.  **Traffic Conditions**: Current congestion and estimated time of arrival (ETA).
3.  **Capacity**: Current occupancy and available tonnes.
4.  **Temperature Requirements**: Availability of specific zones (Ultra-Cold, Frozen, Chilled) matching the cargo type.
5.  **Service Type**: Capability to handle both storage and mechanical reefer repairs.

The selected hub details, including navigation links and contact information, are transmitted directly to the driver via the automated notification system.

**Verification**: Facility availability and status are verified in real-time before any diversion recommendation is issued.

### §4.4 Diversion Fuel & Carbon Calculation

When calculating diversion cost, include:

- Fuel cost: Base rate + distance × ₹15/km
- Driver overtime if applicable
- Hub facility fees (typically ₹2,000-₹5,000/day)
- Carbon footprint: Report CO₂ emissions using 2.68 kg CO₂/liter diesel

**Carbon Offset**: Calculate waste-prevented emissions vs additional fuel emissions.

## §5. Door Management

### §5.1 Door Opening Protocols

Refrigerated cargo doors may only be opened:

- During scheduled loading/unloading at approved facilities
- For emergency inspection (requires manager approval)
- For reefer unit maintenance

**Maximum door open time**: 5 minutes per opening

### §5.2 Door Open Temperature Impact

Expected temperature rise during door operations:

- First minute: +1°C to +2°C
- After 3 minutes: +3°C to +5°C
- After 5 minutes: +5°C to +8°C

Recovery time after door closure: Approximately 15-20 minutes to baseline.

### §5.3 Unauthorized Door Opening

If door open event detected without authorization:

1. Alert driver immediately via radio/phone
2. Verify event with driver
3. Check cargo security and temperature impact
4. Document incident in system
5. If temperature exceeded threshold, follow §3 procedures

## §6. Reefer Unit Maintenance

### §6.1 Preventive Maintenance Schedule

All reefer units must follow preventive maintenance:

- **Daily**: Visual inspection, temperature logs review
- **Weekly**: Compressor operation check, condensate drain check
- **Monthly**: Filter replacement, refrigerant level check
- **Quarterly**: Full system diagnostic, electrical connections inspection
- **Annually**: Complete overhaul and certification

### §6.2 Compressor Failure Indicators

Warning signs of imminent compressor failure:

- Unusual noise or vibration
- Erratic temperature control (±2°C swings)
- Increased power consumption (>20% baseline)
- Refrigerant pressure abnormalities
- Frost accumulation on lines

**Action**: If any 2+ indicators present, schedule immediate maintenance.

### §6.3 Emergency Reefer Repair

If reefer unit fails during transit:

1. Contact nearest authorized repair facility (see hub list §4.3)
2. If repair time >2 hours, initiate diversion to cold storage
3. Document failure mode and symptoms
4. Preserve diagnostic data for warranty claims

## §7. Documentation & Compliance

### §7.1 Incident Documentation Requirements

All temperature excursion incidents must be documented with:

- Event timestamp and duration
- GPS coordinates at alert time
- Temperature reading history (30 min before and after)
- Photos of cargo and reefer unit display
- Actions taken and personnel contacted
- Resolution and outcome

**Retention**: All incident records must be retained for 3 years.

### §7.2 Regulatory Reporting

Temperature excursions must be reported to FSSAI (Food Safety and Standards Authority of India) if:

- Temperature exceeded safe range by >5°C
- Duration exceeded 1 hour
- Cargo is high-risk (meat, seafood, dairy)
- Spoilage detected or suspected

**Reporting Deadline**: Within 24 hours of incident.

### §7.3 Customer Notification

Customers must be notified within:

- 1 hour: For critical excursions (§3.3)
- 4 hours: For moderate excursions requiring diversion
- 24 hours: For minor excursions resolved without impact

## §8. Weather Impact Management

### §8.1 High Ambient Temperature Operations

When ambient temperature exceeds +35°C:

- Reduce door open times by 50%
- Increase monitoring frequency to every 2 minutes
- Pre-cool cargo area before loading
- Avoid midday loading/unloading (11 AM - 3 PM)

### §8.2 Extreme Weather Alerts

Monitor weather forecasts and plan accordingly:

- Heat waves: Consider night-time delivery routes
- Monsoon: Account for traffic delays in ETA calculations
- Winter (northern regions): Monitor for diesel gelling in reefer units

### §8.3 Route Planning

Preferred routes should minimize:

- Time in high ambient temperature zones
- Traffic congestion (reduces reefer efficiency)
- Distance to backup cold storage facilities

## §9. Load Management

### §9.1 Proper Stacking

Improper stacking reduces airflow and cooling efficiency:

- Leave 15 cm clearance from walls and ceiling
- Stack pallets with adequate spacing (minimum 5 cm)
- Never block air circulation vents
- Heavier/denser items on bottom

### §9.2 Load Distribution

Uneven load distribution causes temperature gradients:

- Balance weight across all axles
- Avoid clustering high-value cargo in one zone
- Place temperature-sensitive items near cooling unit
- Use temperature mapping to verify uniformity

### §9.3 Cargo Compatibility

Do not mix incompatible cargo types:

- Strong odors (onions, fish) separate from absorbing foods
- Different temperature requirements in same compartment
- Raw and cooked products

### §9.4 Overloading Prevention

Overloading reduces cooling capacity:

- Never exceed rated cargo capacity by weight
- Never exceed 90% of internal volume
- Account for ice accumulation in freezer units

## §10. Carbon Footprint & Sustainability

### §10.1 Emissions Reporting

Calculate and report CO₂ emissions for all shipments:

- Diesel fuel: 2.68 kg CO₂ per liter
- Electricity (hub storage): 0.82 kg CO₂ per kWh (India grid average)
- Food waste: Use product-specific emission factors (see Appendix B)

### §10.2 Waste Prevention Priority

Preventing food waste is top sustainability priority:

**Carbon Benefit Calculation**:
- Dairy waste prevented: 8.5 kg CO₂e per kg
- Meat waste prevented: 27.0 kg CO₂e per kg
- Seafood waste prevented: 5.1 kg CO₂e per kg
- Vegetables waste prevented: 2.0 kg CO₂e per kg
- Fruits waste prevented: 1.1 kg CO₂e per kg

**Decision Rule**: If diversion prevents waste with CO₂ benefit exceeding diversion fuel emissions by >3:1 ratio, strongly recommend diversion.

### §10.3 Efficiency Improvements

Continuously optimize for lower emissions:

- Regular reefer maintenance reduces power consumption
- Route optimization reduces fuel usage
- Load consolidation improves efficiency
- Use of eco-friendly refrigerants (R-404A replacement)
